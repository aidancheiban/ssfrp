Apply to the Program

SSFRP is a summer research program for high school and college students in the New York City area. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis each spring. See position details below for eligibility requirements.

Team Lead Role

Team Leads help students organize fieldwork, manage research tasks, and stay on track for final presentations alongside Columbia mentors. This is a great opportunity for undergraduate and graduate students looking to gain mentorship experience, develop leadership skills, and contribute to a meaningful research experience for high school students.

Responsibilities

  • Support students during fieldwork and research activities.
  • Help students stay organized and on track with project goals.
  • Assist with communication between students, mentors, and program staff.
  • Support students as they prepare for weekly and final presentations.
  • Attend weekly team lead meetings with program staff reporting on team progress and dynamics.

Eligibility

  • Must be an undergraduate or graduate student.
  • Students from all majors are encouraged to apply.
  • Strong communication and organization skills.
  • Must be 18 years old when the program begins.
  • Reliable transportation to Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory or Columbia University is required. Participants are not eligible for university housing.

Preferred Requirements

  • Studying environmental science, biology, or a related field.
  • Has experience performing scientific research and working in a lab.
  • Knowledge of Google Sheets, Excel, R, Python, and/or other data analysis tools.
  • Taught students in a classroom or laboratory setting.

Time Commitment

  • Participants are expected to commit to the full 6-week summer program.
  • Time commitment: 30-50 hours per week, depending on project scope.
  • Up to 5 online preparation meetings before the program begins.
  • 1 program orientation about a week before the program starts.

Compensation

  • $3,100 one-time stipend.
  • Lunch is provided daily.
  • Prepaid OMNY card to be used on NYC Transit.

Team Member Role

Team Members participate in fieldwork, collect and analyze data, and prepare presentations alongside Columbia mentors and team leads. This is a great opportunity for high school students interested in environmental science, earth science, and hands-on research.

Responsibilities

  • Participate in fieldwork and research activities.
  • Stay organized and on track with project goals.
  • Communicate with team leads, mentors, and program staff.
  • Share weekly project updates with peers and mentors through presentations.
  • Design a scientific poster to be presented at the American Museum of Natural History Symposium.
  • Present a final capstone slide deck at the end of the program.

Eligibility

  • Must be a high school student.
  • Experience working on a team.
  • Has taken the following courses:
    • Regents Living Environment or a biology equivalent
    • Algebra 1 or higher
  • Must be 16 years old when the program begins.
  • Reliable transportation to Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory or Columbia University is required. Participants are not eligible for university housing.

Preferred Requirements

  • Has an interest in studying environmental science, biology, or a related field in college.
  • Has taken the following courses:
    • Regents Chemistry or equivalent
    • Regents Earth Science or equivalent
    • AP Environmental Science
    • AP Chemistry
    • AP Biology
  • Strong presentation and organization skills.
  • Knowledge of Google Sheets, Excel, R, Python, and/or other data analysis tools.

Time Commitment

  • Participants are expected to commit to the full 6-week summer program.
  • Time commitment: 30-50 hours per week, depending on project scope.
  • 1 program orientation about a week before the program starts.

Compensation

  • $1,500 one-time stipend.
  • Lunch is provided daily.
  • Prepaid OMNY card to be used on NYC Transit.
