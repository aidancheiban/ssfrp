Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory

Apply to the Program

SSFRP is a summer research program for high school students in the New York City area. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis each spring.

Who Can Apply

Passionate Science Students in the New York area.

SSFRP is open to current high school and college students who are based in or able to commute to the NYC and Hudson Valley area. The program runs at Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory in Palisades, NY. No prior research experience is required. We look for students with a genuine interest in environmental science, earth science, or the natural world.

The Program

A full summer of real research.

The program runs roughly six to eight weeks each summer. Students join a research team made up of a Columbia or LDEO scientist mentor and an undergraduate or graduate team lead. Each team works on one focused project from start to finish, going through the full research process together.


Past teams have studied microplastics in marsh sediment, methane emissions from wetlands, air quality in the NYC subway, drone-based remote sensing of vegetation, and arsenic removal in drinking water. Students do real fieldwork, run lab analyses, and present their findings to an audience of researchers and faculty at the end of the summer.
