About the Program

The Secondary School Field Research Program gives high school students the chance to conduct real environmental science alongside Columbia University researchers, from fieldwork in the marsh to presentations at LDEO.

Who We Are

Researchers, mentors, and educators.

SSFRP is run by a team of graduate students, postdoctoral researchers, and faculty at Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory, part of Columbia University's Climate School. Our mentors bring expertise across environmental science, geochemistry, remote sensing, and atmospheric science, with a shared commitment to making research accessible to the next generation of scientists. Teams run on a tiered mentoring system, so high school students learn directly from undergraduates, graduate students, and Lamont scientists at the same time.

Our Mission

Real science. Real impact.

SSFRP was created to bridge the gap between classroom science and professional research. Students don't just observe — they design experiments, collect field data, run lab analyses, and contribute to ongoing environmental research at one of the world's leading earth science institutions.


Each team is mentored by Columbia University and LDEO researchers who guide students through the full arc of a research project: forming a hypothesis, gathering evidence, interpreting results, and communicating findings to an audience. Past projects have studied microplastics in marsh sediment, methane emissions from wetlands, drone-based remote sensing, air quality in the NYC subway, and water treatment for arsenic contamination.


By the end of the summer, students leave with hands-on lab and field skills, a completed research project, and experience presenting scientific work in a professional setting.

Program Highlights

What students gain

Image Placeholder

Real Field Skills

Students collect sediment cores, deploy water quality sensors, and run drone-based remote sensing surveys at sites like Piermont Marsh and the Lamont Sanctuary Forest. They leave knowing how to gather and work with real environmental data.

Image Placeholder

Lab and Data Analysis

From measuring microplastics under a microscope to analyzing methane flux and arsenic levels in water samples, students learn to process data the same way researchers do, using the actual instruments and methods in use at LDEO.

Image Placeholder

Scientific Communication

Weekly check-ins and a formal end-of-summer presentation build real public speaking confidence. Students present a scientific poster and slideshow to an audience of researchers, faculty, and peers.
