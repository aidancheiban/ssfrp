# The Secondary School Field Research Program
The Secondary School Field Research Program (SSFRP) is a scientific research program that takes place at Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory. The program is composed of high school students, undergraduates, graduates, and Columbia scientists. The program is broken down into "research teams" that focus on one project. Each team has a scientist that acts as a mentor, undergraduate/graduate student who acts as a team lead ensuring everything is on-time, and high school students who help out and are involved with the research. Projects focus on environmental research in the Piermont Salt March, Lamont Sanctuary Forest, and New York City. Notable projects that came out of this program is the Particulate Matter 2.5 in the NYC Subway.
