About the Program

Students become part of real scientific research.

The Secondary School Field Research Program gives students the opportunity to work with mentors, collect field data, analyze results, and contribute to research connected to Earth and environmental science.


SSFRP runs each summer at Lamont-Doherty Earth Observatory. High school students, undergraduates, and Columbia scientists work in small research teams, with Piermont Marsh in the Hudson estuary serving as the main field site. Each team studies one project from start to finish and presents its findings at the end-of-summer symposium.

Where We Work

Image Placeholder — Lamont Sanctuary Forest

Lamont Sanctuary Forest

A temperate forest in Palisades, NY surrounding the Lamont-Doherty campus. In 2024 the program set up a one-hectare research plot here to track soil and forest health over time.

  • Location Palisades, NY
  • Research plot 1 hectare, since 2024
  • Trees red oak, maple, tulip, white pine
Image Placeholder — Piermont Marsh

Piermont Marsh

A brackish wetland 40 km north of NYC where salt and fresh water mix. It floods 10 to 12 times a month, and its sediment dates back before European arrival in the 1400s. Today the invasive reed Phragmites australis covers most of the marsh surface.

  • Distance 40 km north of NYC
  • Tides floods 10–12 times a month
  • Phragmites cover 90–95% of the surface
Image Placeholder — New York City

New York City

Across the five boroughs and one of the oldest subway systems in the world, the air pollution team has collected PM2.5 data since 2021, connecting air quality to asthma exposure for the more than 8 million people who live here.

  • Population 8 million+
  • Boroughs 5
  • Collecting data since 2021
